OpenClaw is now a Foundation

OpenClaw was created by Peter Steinberger as an open-source platform for personal AI agents ‚Äî software that doesn't just answer questions, but takes action on your behalf. Your email, your calendar, your files, your messages. Managed by AI that works for you.

Now, as Peter joins OpenAI to drive the next generation of personal agents, OpenClaw is becoming something bigger: an independent Foundation dedicated to keeping personal AI open, interoperable, and user-controlled.

OpenAI and Sam Altman will continue to sponsor and support the project. But the Foundation answers to its community ‚Äî not to any single company.

What the Foundation protects

  • Your AI agent runs on your machine, reads your data with your permission, and answers to nobody but you.
  • The codebase stays open source ‚Äî no bait-and-switch, no relicensing, no "open core" compromises.
  • Multiple models, multiple providers. Today's best model shouldn't be tomorrow's lock-in.
  • A skills ecosystem where anyone can build, share, and extend what agents can do.
  • Governance by the community. Corporate sponsors support the project ‚Äî they don't steer it.
  • Privacy isn't a feature. It's the architecture.
